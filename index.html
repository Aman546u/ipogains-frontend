What is an IPO?

An Initial Public Offering (IPO) is the process where a private company sells shares to the public for the first time to raise capital.

How Allotment Works?

Allotment is the process of assigning shares to applicants. It is usually done via a lottery system if the IPO is oversubscribed.

What is GMP?

Grey Market Premium (GMP) is the unofficial price at which IPO shares are traded before they list on the stock exchange.

IPO Risks

Investing in IPOs carries risks. Listing gains are not guaranteed, and prices can fall below the issue price. Always research before key investment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an IPO?

An IPO (Initial Public Offering) is the process through which a private company offers its shares to the public for the first time. After an IPO, the company gets listed on stock exchanges like NSE or BSE, allowing investors to buy and sell shares in the market.

What is IPO GMP?

IPO GMP (Grey Market Premium) is the unofficial premium at which IPO shares are traded before listing. It indicates market demand and investor sentiment, but it is not regulated by SEBI and does not guarantee listing gains.

Is IPO GMP legal in India?

IPO GMP trading happens in the grey market, which is not regulated by SEBI. It is not illegal, but it is unofficial and should only be used for reference while making investment decisions.

How can I check the latest IPO GMP today?

You can check the latest IPO GMP today on ipogains.com, where grey market premium data is updated regularly along with IPO subscription details, issue dates, and listing information.

What is IPO allotment?

IPO allotment is the process of allocating shares to investors after the IPO subscription period ends. If an IPO is oversubscribed, shares are allotted through a computerized lottery system, especially in the retail category.

How to check IPO allotment status?

What is IPO subscription status?

IPO subscription status shows how many times an IPO has been subscribed by different investor categories such as Retail, QIB, and NII during the issue period. It helps investors understand demand for the IPO.

Does high GMP guarantee listing gains?

No, a high IPO GMP does not guarantee listing gains. Listing performance depends on market conditions, company fundamentals, investor sentiment, and overall demand on the listing day.

How many lots can I apply for in an IPO?

Retail investors can apply for IPO shares up to a maximum value of â‚¹2 lakh in a single IPO application. Applications above this amount fall under the HNI category.

What is the IPO price band?

The IPO price band is the price range within which investors can place bids for shares. Investors can bid at the cut-off price to increase chances of allotment in the retail category.

What happens if an IPO is oversubscribed?

When an IPO is oversubscribed, it means demand for shares is higher than the number of shares available. In such cases, retail investors receive allotment through a lottery system.

When are IPO shares credited to the demat account?

IPO shares are usually credited to the investorâ€™s demat account one day before the listing date, after the allotment process is completed and refunds are initiated.

Can I apply for IPOs from multiple demat accounts?

Yes, IPO applications can be made from multiple demat accounts, but each application must have a different PAN number. Multiple applications using the same PAN are rejected.

Is it safe to invest in IPOs?

IPO investments involve market risk. Investors should always review company financials, business model, risks mentioned in the DRHP, and overall market conditions before applying.
